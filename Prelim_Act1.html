My Dream Places to Visit

Number Image Place Description
1 Bukchon Hanok Village in Seoul, South Korea Bukchon Hanok Village is a traditional Korean residential area in Seoul, known for its well-preserved hanoks (traditional Korean houses). Located between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung Palaces, it offers a glimpse into the past with its historic architecture and charming streets. The area is popular with tourists for its cultural attractions, including museums, craft workshops, and cafes located within the hanoks.
2 Mount Fuji in Tokyo, Japan Mount Fuji, also known as Fujisan, is Japan's highest peak, standing at 3,776 meters (12,388 feet). It is a dormant volcano and a UNESCO World Heritage site, renowned for its symmetrical cone shape and cultural significance. Located southwest of Tokyo, it's a popular destination for hiking, spiritual pilgrimages, and artistic inspiration.
3 Zurich, Switzerland Zurich is located in the heart of Europe and is an important economic, cultural and social centre of Switzerland. With its unique location on Lake Zurich, the Alps on the horizon, the numerous local recreation areas and its urban diversity, the city offers an attractive environment to live and work.
4 Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II in Milan, Italy Galleria Vittorio Emanuele II is an elegant, historic shopping arcade in Milan, Italy, renowned for its stunning architecture and luxurious atmosphere. It's a four-story, cross-shaped structure with a glass and iron dome, often called "Milan's drawing room" for its central role in the city's social life. The Galleria houses high-end boutiques, restaurants, and cafes, and is a popular destination for both locals and tourists.
5 Hakone-Jinja Shrine in Hakone, Japan Hakone Shrine (箱根神社, Hakone Jinja) stands at the foot of Mount Hakone along the shores of Lake Ashinoko. The shrine buildings are hidden in the dense forest, but are well advertised by its huge torii gates, one standing prominently in the lake and two others over the main street of Moto-Hakone.
6 Machu Picchu in Urubamba Province, Peru Machu Picchu is a 15th-century Inca citadel nestled high in the Andes Mountains of Peru, renowned for its stunning location, sophisticated architecture, and historical significance. Often called the "Lost City of the Incas," it's a UNESCO World Heritage site and one of the New Seven Wonders of the World.
7 Moai Statues in Easter Island, Chile Moai statues are massive, monolithic human figures carved from volcanic rock by the indigenous Rapa Nui people on Easter Island. These iconic statues, also known as "Easter Island heads," are known for their large heads, heavy brows, elongated noses, and thin, protruding lips. They are typically found standing on platforms called "ahu" with their backs to the sea, facing inland.
8 Basílica de la Sagrada Família in Barcelona, Spain The Sagrada Familia is an unfinished, iconic basilica in Barcelona, Spain, designed by Antoni Gaudí. Its unique blend of Gothic and Art Nouveau architectural styles, along with its intricate symbolism and naturalistic forms, makes it a globally recognized landmark. Construction began in 1882, and while projected to be completed in 2026, the timeline has been adjusted.
9 Chocolate Hills in Bohol, Philippines The Chocolate Hills in Bohol, Philippines, are a unique geological formation characterized by over 1,200 symmetrical, cone-shaped hills spread across a vast area. These hills are primarily covered in grass, which turns brown during the dry season, giving them a chocolate-like appearance, hence their name.
10 Boracay Beach in Aklan, Philippines Boracay is a tropical island in the Philippines known for its stunning white sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant nightlife. It's a popular destination for relaxation and adventure, offering activities like swimming, island hopping, diving, and kitesurfing. The island is located about 315 kilometers south of Manila and is part of the Western Visayas region.
